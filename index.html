Self-Evolving Curriculum for Multi-Agent Path Finding

An LLM analyses its own failures and rewrites the next-round training distribution — yielding a curriculum that emerges purely from RL feedback.

Abstract

We study whether a large language model can author its own training curriculum for multi-agent path finding (MAPF). After each round of GRPO-based reinforcement learning, an analyst LLM is presented with the per-map-size failure breakdown and re-weights the next-round sampling distribution by editing a single YAML config. With a fixed per-round sample budget of 4 000 trajectories, the model steadily allocates more capacity to the failure modes it cannot yet solve, and the resulting policy achieves substantially higher valid and optimal rates than uniform-curriculum baselines on held-out 3–10×10 boards.

Case Study

A 3×3 board, two agents. Both models receive the same prompt; we visualise the executed plan token-by-token. The base model invents an illegal trajectory; the self-trained model recovers the optimal makespan-2 solution.
